CMT301 - Business and IT Management

Taught by: Dr W. K. Ivins

Outline Description of Module

Information systems are usually a major business investment so there is a need to ensure they are appropriately designed, developed, implemented and managed in order to ensure that the expected business benefits are achieved. It is important that Information Systems evolve to ensure they can continue to support the organisation’s needs. This module aims to develop the technical, business and management understanding to enable individuals to define and justify effective information systems to support business activities. It also provides an awareness of the skills necessary to manage projects, and knowledge of the relevant human and organisational factors that can influence the successful introduction of and subsequent support of information systems.Students will apply their knowledge and skills in a business change project which will involve team and individual tasks.
In addition, the module aims to equip students with an awareness of current trends in the development of information systems, and to identify and critically evaluate the factors involved in selecting the most suitable combination of technology, development and management approach to meet the organisation’s requirements.

On completion of the module a student should be able to

  1. Understand the nature of organisations and the people within them, and their use of information for strategic and operational business purposes.
  2. Research and critically evaluate current trends and developments in constructing Information Systems and assess their impact on the strategy of organisations
  3. Determine and specify requirements for a change project to meet new business needs
  4. Design appropriate business processes and data architectures to support these requirements
  5. Evaluate approaches to choose an appropriate software development approach for the change project
  6. Appreciate how the choice of development approach and architecture can affect quality
  7. Apply appropriate project management techniques to plan and cost the implementation of the change project
  8. Determine legal, ethical, business and technical risks in developing the new information system and determine appropriate recommendations for mitigating these risks.
  9. Analyse and reflect on their personal performance and the team’s performance in carrying out the tasks in the change project.

CMT302 - E-Commerce and Innovation

Taught by: Mrs N. Edwards

Outline Description of Module

The advent of the internet has created many new business opportunities. This module aims to equip students with an understanding of these new business opportunities and how to approach the tasks associated with researching and setting up a high-tech business or social enterprise venture.
This module will explore the business models, techniques and underpinning technologies associated with E-Commerce using a case based approach. The meaning of “Innovation” will be explored, including how this can be promoted and nurtured. The entrepreneurship skills associated with the set up and running of a new high-tech business or social enterprise will be explored by means of the production of a business plan.

On completion of the module a student should be able to

  1. Understand the concept of Innovation and what this means within the context of the IT industry.
  2. Understand the concepts, techniques and underpinning technologies associated with E-Commerce in the modern environment.
  3. Demonstrate understanding of entrepreneurial activities, and critically analyse and appraise business opportunities.
  4. Understand the different types of Intellectual Property and their value to an organisation.
  5. Appreciate risks involved in managing a high-tech enterprise, including concepts and methodologies for information risk assessment, assurance and management.
  6. Understand the role and importance of securing systems, and impact and consequences of security breaches and data loss.
  7. Understand research methods and their role in innovation.
  8. Understand and utilise a variety of quantitative and qualitative research methodologies, methods and tools.
  9. Write and present scientific and technical material to different audiences.
  10. Plan, write and present a business plan for a new company or social enterprise.

CMT103 - Information Processing in Python

Taught by: Dr C. Walker

Outline Description of Module

The aim of this module is to introduce techniques to extract, store and process information using the Python programming language. To achieve this, students will be introduced to fundamental data structures and algorithms, the design and use of databases, regular expressions and the basics of HTML.

On completion of the module a student should be able to

  1. Use Python to extract, manipulate, store and analyse information from a range of sources.
  2. Apply regular expressions.
  3. Contrast common algorithms for searching and sorting.
  4. Contrast, select and use data structures appropriate to a given problem.
  5. Design, implement and utilise relational databases.

CMT207 - Information Modelling & Database Systems

Taught by: Mr T. Vanasescu

Outline Description of Module

Database systems are the most widely used software systems in commerce and industry. Database management systems are used to store and manage the complex integrated information resources of organisations. This module introduces the theoretical and practical issues relating to the design and use of these systems. In addition to the provision of a sound foundation in traditional, second generation database systems, it explores the representation and management of complex information resources with third generation database technology. The module highlights how database systems are at the core of modern information-rich Web sites, and explores the role of XML, JSON and Semantic Web technologies in creating a “Web of data”. Systems for data mining and business intelligence will be discussed.

On completion of the module a student should be able to

  1. Understand how to map conceptual models to efficient representations in a database schema.
  2. Understand the latest types of DBMS – relational, object oriented, object- relational, non-relational, cloud, distributed.
  3. Understand the role of database systems in information-rich Websites.
  4. Understand how XML and Semantic Web technologies are used to model. and manage information in a Web context
  5. Understand how significant relationships and trends can be mined from data

CMT202 - Distributed and Cloud Computing

Taught by: Dr I. M. Cooper

Outline Description of Module

The aim of this module is to familiarize students with issues and successful approaches in the design of distributed systems. Detailed case studies of widely used systems will be discussed in this module.
The course covers: the organisation of distributed systems, focusing on various architectural styles used to develop such systems; core technologies to implement distributed systems; various models and infrastructures to support Cloud computing – such as virtualisation; and emerging themes in distributed systems, such as fault tolerance and policy driven autonomic self-management.

On completion of the module a student should be able to

  1. Have knowledge about the state-of-the-art in distributed-systems architectures.
  2. Understand issues in distributing an application across a network.
  3. Appreciate the difference between various distributed computing middleware and their communication mechanisms.
  4. Have knowledge of common security practices within distributed systems.
  5. Understand and be able to utilize Cloud computing environments.
  6. Knowledge of standards in distributed computing, and the impact of standardisation on application programs.

CMT206 - Human Centric Computing

Taught by: Dr A. I. Abdelmoty

Outline Description of Module

As information systems become more pervasive and complex there is a growing need to ensure these systems are fit for purpose as far as the end user communities are concerned. As delivery platforms proliferate to include smart phones, tablets and mobile games consoles as well as traditional desktop systems, the interaction mechanisms and contexts in which these interactions take place also expand. This module aims to develop the technical, societal, business and management understanding to enable individuals to define and deliver effective information systems from a human centric perspective.
This module takes a systems approach to Human Centric Computing and deals with relevant aspects of Human Computer Interaction (HCI), Visualization and Virtual Environments.

On completion of the module a student should be able to

  1. Understand the complex nature of users and their interaction with artifacts and systems.
  2. Describe the characteristics of human perception, the limitations of human information processing, and the relation between perception and human cognition in computer systems and visualizations.
  3. Appreciate the importance and context of HCI and human factors in the software development lifecycle and for the successful introduction of interactive computer systems.
  4. Select and apply suitable usability evaluation methodologies for the evaluation of interactive software systems.
  5. Develop and evaluate competing proposals for interface design and implementation.
  6. Understand and apply human centric design methodologies in the context of current and emerging interaction technologies.
  7. Understand the method of scientific analysis, the control of variables, analysis, and the presentation of outcomes.
  8. Understand the importance of the correct deployment of visualization techniques to convey complex information.
  9. Discuss the challenges and opportunities presented by visualization and interaction methods in virtual environments, virtual reality, augmented reality, and pervasive computing.
